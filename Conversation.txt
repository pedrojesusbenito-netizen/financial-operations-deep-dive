# Why #5: Decision-Safe vs Decision-Unsafe G&A Classification

## A. Decision-Safe G&A Components

### Defining Characteristics
1. Near-zero offset ratio
2. External counterparty
3. Transactional clarity

### Categories Exhibiting Decision-Safe Patterns
- Outsourced Services
- Hosting / Infrastructure
- External Contractors

### Why These Do Not Depend on Allocation Mechanics
- Represent actual economic outflows
- Not subject to internal reallocation
- Gross recording equals economic reality

## B. Decision-Unsafe G&A Components

### Defining Characteristics
1. High offset ratio
2. Internal or allocative nature
3. Structural reversal patterns

### Categories Exhibiting Decision-Unsafe Patterns
- Personnel / Compensation
- Occupancy / Facilities
- Benefits
- Commissions

### Why Offsets Materially Distort Economic Reality
- Net figure may not represent true economic cost
- Observable net is an accounting residual

### Risks of Premature Action
- Misattribution
- Double-counting or under-counting
- False precision
- Unintended consequences

## C. Hard Boundaries of This P&L

### What This Dataset Can Definitively Support
1. Identification of offset behavior by category and department
2. Classification of spend into persistence vs reversal buckets
3. Magnitude of gross-to-net reduction
4. Structural vs random pattern determination

### What This Dataset Fundamentally Cannot Answer
1. Why reversals occur
2. Whether reversals are correct
3. Period-over-period consistency
4. Direction of allocation flows
5. True economic cost of high-offset categories

## D. Implications for Next-Phase Diligence (Non-Prescriptive)

Types of visibility required:
1. Allocation methodology documentation
2. Intercompany or shared-services agreements
3. Prior-period P&L comparison
4. Journal entry detail for high-offset categories
5. Cost center or entity hierarchy

## Closing Statement
This analysis establishes a clean stopping point. The P&L data supports
confident action on decision-safe categories. It does not support confident
action on decision-unsafe categories where offset mechanics materially distort
the relationship between recorded and economic cost.
